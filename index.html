👋 Hello! Don't worry, you are safe here.

This page is here to show you, in plain language, exactly what information websites can see about you. Nothing here is stored or sent anywhere. It's just for your eyes! Think of it like looking in a mirror.

📍 Where you are in the world
🌐
Your IP Address
Your Internet "Home Address"
Looking this up…
Every device on the internet has an IP address — like a home address, but for data. Websites can see it automatically. This tells them roughly which city or region you're in.
🗺️
Approximate Location
Your Estimated City & Country
Detecting…
From your IP address alone, websites can usually guess your city, region, and country. They don't need you to type it in!
🏢
Internet Service Provider
Who Provides Your Internet
Detecting…
Your ISP is the company you pay for internet. Websites can see your provider just from your IP address.
🕐
Time Zone
What Clock You're Living By
—
Your device automatically tells websites your time zone. This helps confirm where in the world you are, even if you're using a VPN.
💻 Your device & browser
🌍
Browser
Which App You're Using to Browse
—
Whether Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge — websites know immediately.
📱
Device Type
Phone, Tablet, or Computer?
—
Websites can tell what type of device you're on so they can adjust how the page looks for you.
🖥️
Screen Size
How Big Your Screen Is
—
Your exact screen size in pixels. Combined with other info, this helps identify your device even across different websites.
💡
Operating System
Windows, Mac, iPhone, or Android?
—
Your operating system is part of what your browser automatically tells every website you visit.
🌐
Language
What Language Your Device Is Set To
—
Websites see your preferred language instantly. They use this to decide which language to show you.
🔋
Battery Level
How Much Charge Your Device Has
Checking…
Yes, really! Some websites can see how much battery you have. Some companies have even used this to charge higher prices to people with low batteries.
📶
Internet Connection
How You're Connected
—
Websites can often tell if you're on WiFi, mobile data, or a cable — and roughly how fast your connection is.
⚙️ Your browser preferences
🚫
Do Not Track
Have You Asked Not to Be Tracked?
—
Your browser can send a "please don't track me" signal. Most websites ignore it completely — there's no law forcing them to obey it.
🌓
Dark / Light Mode
Your Colour Scheme Preference
—
Your display preference is visible to every website. It's one more small detail that makes your fingerprint unique.
🛡️
Ad Blocker
Are You Blocking Adverts?
Checking…
Websites quietly check whether you're using an ad blocker. Some sites refuse to load unless you turn it off.
👆
Touchscreen
Can You Tap the Screen?
—
Websites know whether your device has a touchscreen. Useful for adapting the interface, but also part of your fingerprint.
🧠
CPU Cores
How Powerful Your Processor Is
—
The number of CPU cores in your device is readable by websites. Another detail that contributes to your unique device fingerprint.
💾
RAM
How Much Memory Your Device Has
—
An approximate amount of RAM is exposed to websites. Combined with CPU, screen, and GPU info, it narrows down your exact device model.
👀 What you've done so far
⏱️
Time on Page
How Long You've Been Here
0 seconds
Websites track exactly how long you spend on each page — including every scroll, pause, and return visit.
🖱️
Mouse Position
Where Your Cursor Is Right Now
Move your mouse…
Websites can track your exact cursor position in real time. Mouse movement patterns are unique enough to identify you.
📜
Scroll Depth
How Far Down You've Read
0%
Every pixel you scroll is tracked. Advertisers use this to know whether you actually read an article — or just opened it.
↩️
Referrer
The Last Website You Were On
—
When you click a link to visit a website, that site can see which website sent you. This is called the "referrer".
🧬 Your digital fingerprint
🔢
Browser Fingerprint
Your Unique Browser ID
Calculating…
By combining all the information above, websites can create a unique ID for your device. This works even if you clear your cookies.
🎨
Canvas Fingerprint
Your Browser's Unique Drawing Style
Rendering…
Your graphics hardware renders this tiny image in a way subtly different from every other device. The pixel differences become your fingerprint.
🖥️
WebGL — Graphics Card
What's Doing the Drawing Inside Your Device
—
Your GPU name is readable by every website through WebGL. Very few people share the exact same GPU and driver version.
🎵
Audio Fingerprint
Your Device's Unique Sound Signature
Calculating…
Your browser silently processes a tiny piece of audio (you won't hear anything). The way your hardware handles sound is microscopically unique.
🔤
Installed Fonts
Which Text Styles Are on Your Device
Detecting…
The fonts on your device depend on your OS, region, and apps. The exact combination is surprisingly unique.
🍪
Cookies
Tiny Files Stored on Your Device
—
Cookies are small files websites leave on your device. They remember who you are next time you visit. Some cookies track you across many different websites.
📦
Local Storage
More Data Saved on Your Device
—
Like cookies but bigger. Websites can store even more data on your device — including tracking IDs that survive cookie deletion.

🤔 So what does all this mean?

Websites collect this information for different reasons. Some are helpful, some are a bit sneaky.

🛍️

Shopping Sites

Track what you looked at to show you ads for the same things elsewhere.

📰

News Sites

Track what you read to guess your political views and interests.

📱

Social Media

Collect the most data — your friends, opinions, location, and behaviour.

🔍

Search Engines

Remember every search you've ever typed to build a profile about you.
